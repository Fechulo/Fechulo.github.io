My Instrument Collection

Over the years I've accumulated a number of musical instruments, only some of which I can actually play. This page is a showcase of all the instruments I own and some information about each one.

Yamaha TRBX304 Bass

When people ask what instrument I play, I usually say bass guitar, as this is by far the instrument I've played the most in recent years. I only got Ash a few years ago (Yes, I do name some of my instruments, very pretentious, I know) after spending a long time playing basslines on my (far too high-pitched) guitars. Although I don't like the stereotype of bad guitar players becoming mediocre bass players, that's exactly what happened in my case...

Classical Guitar

First music instrument I learned to play back in 2009. This one actually belonged to my dad. I had a much smaller guitar back then but I grew and it didn't.

"Gibson" Les Paul Electric Guitar

It took a fair bit of convincing, but my parents eventually got me an electric guitar, which I named Ellie. Although not an actual Gibson, Ellie is most certainly a stunning instrument. I don't play her very often anymore. In fact, she's been missing a high E string for quite some time

Epiphone Acoustic Guitar

This one doesn't belong to me actually but its real owner has yet to notice its absence. I "borrowed" it for a Music Competition I took part in (and won) in Prague. I used this instrument for a song that required a guitar in open G tuning, so I bought some very high-tension strings that could survive a significant drop in tension. Unfortunately, they make the guitar practically unplayable in standard E tuning.

Laka Ukulele

Onto the last of my named instruments. Behold Lala. After becoming obsessed with the idea of owning a Ukulele a few years back, I set out to get myself a Uke for under €30 and ended up getting this beautifully resonant Ukulele for €99... Thanks to its size, I've actually been able to bring Lala on some of my trips, having traveled as far as South America!

Kawai X30 Keyboard

Moving away from the plucked strings section, this is my keyboard. It belonged to my late grandmother originally, who passed it on to my father who eventually (sort of?) passed it on to me. I've recently been teaching myself some music theory and keyboards are by far the best instruments for this. Unfortunately I can't play very well but I have been learning Erik Satie's "Gymnopédie No.1" in an attempt to get better at piano.

Hohner 32-key Melodica
